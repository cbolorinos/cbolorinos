Saturno Rebranding

Shortly after arriving at Saturno I was asked to rebrand the company. I aimed for a few years behind the cutting edge of design, as our clients were law firms, not start ups, and wanted something a little more tried and true.


The orange was part of the original brand which I kept. They also had blue in the original brand, which I kept, but dialed back quite a lot. It can be seen in the vertical line going through the letterhead.


While I begged to give the entire website a makeover, we never found the time. However, I did get a chance to redesign the slides of the hero banner.

Barclay Damon Website.

Cohne Kinghorn Website

This is a fully responsive website for a Utah law firm called Cohne Kinghorn. Originally we only were supposed to redesign the hero banner and maybe the fonts/colors, as they were a low budget job. However, that week I had a lot of spare time, and I had been itching to try a split screen layout, as multiple graphic design blogs mentioned they were gaining popularity.


I turned in three designs on the deadline. Two of them were slight variations of Cohne's design, which was very outdated, so I wont post those here.


As for the third option, well, it's the one you're looking at! They liked it so much that they asked to have it built, even though it was about 400% of the budget they had originally planned for. Needless to say my boss was pleased, and needless to say, I was very proud of myself!

OCP Equity Partners Website

This was for a client who wanted an HTML mockup of their website. The original HTML mockup can be found on my github.


All I was given was their original logo, the icon on the top right. They had vitually no design assets or guidelines, as their company had mostly advertised through word of mouth until then. I designed a geometric hexagon theme, and it has now become an integral part of their brand.


Like all of my websites, it is fully responsive. Current website can be seen here.

Caroline Hanssen

Caroline Hanssen is a high end tutor in Marin, California. She hired me because she wanted a rebranding, business cards and a website that completely broke the mold. I was under instructions to not use apples, pencils, graduation caps, or any other symbol of education. I was also told to make a completely unusual and modern layout.


I settled on a cinemagraph owl. Mildly related to education, but mostly just quirky, original and ornate.
I built the site using Bootstrap, and you can view it here.
